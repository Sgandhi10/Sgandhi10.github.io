TJ REVERB

The TJ Space program is a high school club working with individuals from GMU, NASA, and other organizations to build and design cubesats. TJ REVERB is a 2U cubesat that was launched in November 29th, 2022 on SpaceX CRS-26 and deployed December 29th from the International Space Station(ISS). The cubesat is a 2U satellite that will be used to test the Iridium network in space. The Iridium network is a network of satellites that provide global communication. Currently, the Iridium network is primarily used on the surface of Earth, but the TJ REVERB mission will test the network in space. The Iridium network will also be utilized to geolocate the cubesat's position in space based on relative location to satellites. The cubesat is also equipped with an APRS radio that will be used as the secondary radio system for communication between the cubesat and the ground station.
